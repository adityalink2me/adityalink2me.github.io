The Mystery

A confident answer without evidence starts the mystery.

1. The question

Maya, a teacher, asks: “Does screen time always harm learning?” An AI gives a polished answer.

2. The uncomfortable pause

Which studies? How recent? Which ages? What do experts disagree about?

3. The detective move

Maya needs an answer she can inspect, challenge, explain, and responsibly share.

Class question: Would you use an answer in a meeting if you could not show where it came from?

Field Method

A strong Perplexity session follows six visible moves.

1Ask

Make the question precise.

2Select

Choose useful source types.

3Read

Scan the answer and claims.

4Trace

Open the citations.

5Follow

Probe gaps and disagreements.

6Save

Store the useful trail.

The answer is only step three. Verification is where research becomes trustworthy.

Prompt Anatomy

Better research prompts specify question, context, evidence, and output.

Context

Evidence

Output

Test itFor school leaders in Nepal, explain three evidence-based ways to reduce teacher administrative workload. Use sources from 2024 onward, note disagreements, and end with a five-point action list.

Prompt Laboratory

One topic can reveal five different Perplexity capabilities.

CurrentWhat are the latest recommendations on teaching AI literacy in schools? Cite sources and show publication dates.
CompareCompare two viewpoints on homework effectiveness. Where do the sources disagree?
AcademicFind recent academic evidence on retrieval practice. Explain it for a beginner teacher.
Follow-upWhich claim in your answer has the weakest evidence? Explain why.
SynthesizeTurn the strongest findings into a 150-word staff briefing with citations.

Lab rule

After every answer, inspect at least two citations. Record one source you trust and one you reject.

Run the prompts in orderSlide 9
Allow 20–25 minutes. Pairs may divide the prompts and compare results.

Build the Room

Custom instructions make the Space behave like a careful research assistant.

Space instructionsYou support teachers at a secondary school in Nepal. Prefer sources from governments, universities, and recognized organizations. Always show publication dates. Separate evidence from suggestions. Use simple English and flag uncertainty.

Three instruction layers

  1. Identity: who the Space supports
  2. Evidence rules: preferred sources, dates, exclusions
  3. Answer style: tone, length, language, format

Pairs present one finding, one citation, and one limitationSlide 20
Score usefulness, source quality, honest uncertainty, and clarity rather than presentation polish alone.
Finish Strong

The goal is not faster answers. It is faster, inspectable understanding.

Ask a sharper question, trace the evidence, verify before trusting, and share conclusions with honest limits.

Exit ticket

  1. Name one citation you trust.
  2. Name one citation you rejected.
  3. Explain why you made each decision.
  4. State one thing you still need to verify.
