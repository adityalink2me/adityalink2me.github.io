Why We Are Not Jumping Ahead

Seeing a skill once is not the same as owning it.

Recognition

"That looks familiar." Useful, but weak.

Recall

"I can explain it without notes." Stronger.

Application

"I can use it in a real situation." Better.

Judgment

"I know when to use it and when not to." Mastery.

Today's goal: move every important idea from "I saw it" to "I can use it."

Revision Station 1 | AI Basics

AI predicts useful output. It does not “know” like a careful human.

Good at

Drafting, summarizing, transforming, brainstorming, pattern work.

Can fail at

Facts, context, judgment, fresh information, and hidden assumptions.

Human job

Set the goal, provide context, check, decide, and take responsibility.

Golden rule

Helpful output is a draft until verified for its real use.

Fun game: AI or not?

Mime one task. Your team decides:

AI can assistHuman must decideUse both

Try: write a poem, approve a loan, diagnose illness, summarize notes, choose a punishment.

Capability and responsibility are different questionsSlide 5
The best answer is often “use both,” but learners must describe each role.

Revision Station 5 | Safe and Responsible AI
Accuracy

Verify important facts, calculations, claims, and answer keys.

Privacy

Remove personal, confidential, medical, financial, or protected data.

Fairness

Check stereotypes, exclusions, assumptions, and unequal effects.

Ownership

Respect sources, copyright, permission, and human accountability.

Red flag race

A manager pastes employee medical records into a public chatbot and asks it to decide who should be promoted. Find at least five problems and suggest a safer workflow.

Higher stakes require stronger human reviewSlide 9
Expected themes: privacy, fairness, unsupported decision making, transparency, policy, and qualified human review.
Revision Station 6 | Professional Documents

Build editable first. Polish second. Export last.

1Brief

Goal, audience, facts.

2Outline

Sections and logic.

3Draft

Editable content.

4Review

Accuracy and design.

5Export

PDF for sharing.

Practical decision

Your organization needs a partnership proposal that three people must edit before approval.

Best workflow: draft and revise in an editable format such as DOCX or Google Docs. Export PDF only after the final review.
A PDF is usually a final sharing format, not a drafting formatSlide 10

Revision Station 7 | Research and Citations

A citation is an invitation to check, not proof by itself.

Claim

What exact statement is being made?

Support

Does the source support that exact claim?

Quality

Is the source credible for this question?

Date

Is it current enough?

Source detective

Question: “What improves student attendance?” Rank what you would open first:

  1. Recent systematic review
  2. Education ministry report
  3. Anonymous social post
  4. Headline with no linked study
There can be multiple useful sources, but broad claims need stronger evidence than one person's experience.
Trace important claims back to their evidenceSlide 11
Let teams argue for their ranking. The reasoning matters more than one rigid order.
Revision Station 8 | NotebookLM

Control the source pack before asking questions.

Curate

Choose relevant, trustworthy sources.

Question

Orient, locate, connect, challenge.

Trace

Open citations and inspect context.

Create

Turn understanding into study outputs.

Best use?

You have six approved policy documents and need staff to compare rules, locate exceptions, and build a study guide using only those files.

NotebookLM fits well because the task begins with a controlled source collection. Grounding reduces wandering, but important claims still need checking.
Source-first, not answer-firstSlide 12
Ask how this differs from starting with the open web.
Revision Station 9 | Interactive HTML

HTML, CSS, and JavaScript have different jobs.

HTML

Structure and meaning: headings, text, buttons, questions.

CSS

Appearance: color, spacing, layout, typography.

JavaScript

Behavior: clicks, scoring, feedback, shuffle, restart.

Bug detective

The quiz looks beautiful, but clicking an answer does nothing.

First inspect the JavaScript behavior. Then test nearby features such as score, feedback, next question, and restart after the fix.
Test 0%Test 100%Test restart
Test content, behavior, and experienceSlide 13
Ask learners why a beautiful activity can still be a poor learning experience.

Fun Game | Explain It Without the Word

Can your team identify the idea?

One person explains a concept without using the forbidden words. The team has 30 seconds to guess.

Prompt

Forbidden: instruction, ask, AI

Citation

Forbidden: source, link, proof

Privacy

Forbidden: personal, secret, data

HTML

Forbidden: webpage, tag, structure

Bias

Forbidden: unfair, stereotype, prejudice

Iteration

Forbidden: improve, repeat, revise

Why this works

If you can explain an idea in fresh language and give an example, you probably understand it beyond memorized words.

Team Challenge | Workflow Relay

Scenario

Your school wants a short, evidence-based proposal for improving attendance. It must use recent web research, compare three existing school documents, protect student privacy, and end as a polished PDF.

Time: 8 minutesEvery teammate adds one step

Build the workflow

  1. Define the decision and safe evidence needed.
  2. Research current evidence and verify citations.
  3. Compare approved internal documents.
  4. Draft an editable proposal.
  5. Review facts, fairness, privacy, and design.
  6. Export final PDF after approval.
Challenge: name the best tool or format at every step and explain why.
Strong work combines skills instead of using one tool for everythingSlide 16
This is an integrated practice task, not a project build.

Hard Round Debrief

Can you make a defensible decision under uncertainty?

ChallengeAn AI-generated briefing cites five sources and sounds professional. Two sources are old, one citation does not support its claim, one claim comes only from a social post, and the conclusion recommends a high-stakes policy change.

Your response must include

  1. What can still be used?
  2. What must be checked or removed?
  3. What evidence is missing?
  4. Who should review the decision?
  5. How should uncertainty be communicated?
Time: 7 minutes

Project Readiness Gate

We are ready for projects when we can do these reliably.

Frame

State a useful goal and audience.

Choose

Select a fitting tool and workflow.

Prompt

Give context, format, and criteria.

Verify

Check facts, evidence, safety, and fairness.

Improve

Test, observe, and revise precisely.

Readiness is not a perfect quiz score. It is knowing how to notice a problem and make the next responsible move.
