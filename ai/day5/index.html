Safe and Responsible AI Use

AI can be useful, fast, and convincing. Today you learn how to protect information, notice problems, verify important claims, and stay responsible for the final work.

Core Principle

AI suggests. Humans decide.

The person who sends the email, submits the report, teaches the lesson, or publishes the post remains responsible.

AI can draft

Ideas, wording, structure, examples, summaries, and starting points.

Humans must check

Facts, context, tone, fairness, privacy, and whether the answer is appropriate.

Humans approve

The final decision, final output, and consequences.

Responsibility cannot be outsourced to a chatbotSlide 3
Connect this principle to their real roles.
Incorrect Answers

AI can invent a believable answer.

Sometimes an AI system produces false details, fake references, outdated information, or an answer that does not match the question.

False factsFake sourcesWrong calculationsOutdated rules

Warning signs

  • Exact claims with no source
  • A citation you cannot open or find
  • An answer that ignores local context
  • Confident language about a high-stakes decision
  • Different answers after asking the same question
Fluent language is not proofSlide 4

Five-Step Verification

Pause before you publish or act.

1Identify

Underline the claims, dates, numbers, names, and citations.

2Ask

Request sources and uncertainty, but do not stop there.

3Search

Use Perplexity, official websites, or trusted documents.

4Compare

Check at least two reliable sources for important claims.

5Correct

Edit the output and record the source you relied on.

Protect Information

Do not paste sensitive information into public AI tools.

Treat a public chatbot like an external service. Follow your school or organization's policy and use anonymized examples whenever possible.

Remove namesRemove IDsRemove passwordsRemove confidential records

Never share without clear approval

  • Passwords, access codes, and financial details
  • Student or employee personal records
  • Health, disciplinary, or confidential information
  • Unpublished documents or restricted data
  • Private photos, messages, or contact lists
When uncertain, stop and askSlide 8
Discuss the organization's real data policy if one exists.
Safer Practice

Unsafe prompt

Analyze these student marks and write a report for parents: [real student names, IDs, phone numbers, and marks].

Safer prompt

Using anonymized class data, summarize overall performance and learning gaps. Use labels such as Student A and Student B. Do not include personal identifiers.
Minimum dataPlaceholdersGrouped results

Bias and Fairness

AI can repeat unfair patterns.

Outputs may reflect stereotypes, missing viewpoints, or assumptions from training data and the prompt.

Ask who is missing

Does the answer represent different people and experiences?

Challenge assumptions

Does it assume a role belongs to one gender, class, age, or culture?

Use clear criteria

For selection or evaluation, define fair and job-relevant standards.

Keep human review

Do not let AI make final high-impact decisions about people.

Fairness needs active reviewSlide 10
Use simple non-sensitive examples, such as stereotyped job descriptions.
Prompt for Fairer Review

Try this review prompt

Review this draft for stereotypes, unfair assumptions, missing viewpoints, and language that may exclude people. Explain each concern and suggest a fairer revision. Do not make a final decision about any person.

Human questions still matter

  • Is the task appropriate for AI?
  • Are the criteria relevant and transparent?
  • Could someone be harmed or excluded?
  • Who should review the result?
  • Can a person challenge the decision?
AI can help find bias, but it cannot guarantee fairnessSlide 11
Emphasize that a bias-check prompt is only one layer.
Copyright and Ownership

AI-generated does not mean risk-free.

Respect original creators, licenses, school rules, and organizational policies. Be especially careful when publishing, selling, or presenting work as your own.

Use your own material

Start with content you created or have permission to use.

Transform thoughtfully

Add real judgment, editing, context, and original value.

Credit sources

Cite important research, images, quotes, and borrowed ideas.

When the stakes are commercial or legal, check the rulesSlide 12
Avoid giving legal certainty. Encourage checking local rules and licenses.
Do Not Use AI to Deceive
Fake evidence

Do not create fake citations, testimonials, records, certificates, or achievements.

Impersonation

Do not pretend to be another person or create misleading messages in their name.

Cheating

Use AI to learn and improve, not to hide work you did not do.

Manipulation

Do not use AI to mislead people with false images, claims, or promises.

Simple test: Would you be comfortable explaining exactly how AI helped create this work?

Transparency protects trustSlide 13
Invite examples from education, office work, and social media.
Match Checking to Risk
Low risk

Brainstorming names, planning a game, drafting a personal checklist.

  • Quick review
  • Edit for usefulness
Medium risk

Public posts, reports, teaching materials, customer messages.

  • Check facts and tone
  • Review privacy and copyright
High risk

Health, legal, financial, disciplinary, hiring, grading, or safety decisions.

  • Qualified human review
  • Official sources and policy
  • Do not rely on AI alone
