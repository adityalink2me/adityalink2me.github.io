Why Tool Choice Matters

The same prompt can feel different in different assistants.

One tool may give a fast list. Another may write a longer polished draft. Another may connect better with your Google or Microsoft files.

Click the situation closest to your work.

Layman rule

A tool is like a helper with a habit. Learn the habit, then choose the helper that fits the job.

Choose by task, file location, output style, and safetySlide 3
Avoid declaring one winner. Keep the mindset: right fit for the task.
One-Minute Decision Rule
What do I need?

Idea, email, table, report, code, slide, analysis, or assistant?

Where is my work?

Chat, Google Docs, Microsoft Word, PDF, spreadsheet, or browser?

How deep is it?

Quick answer, long document, many files, or repeat workflow?

What is sensitive?

Private records, student data, company files, names, IDs, or passwords?

Tool List 1

ChatGPT is a strong all-purpose starting point.

Use it when you do not know where to start, need a fast draft, want multiple options, or need help thinking through a task.

Productivity

Plans, checklists, schedules, emails, meeting notes.

Creative work

Names, captions, poster copy, lesson ideas, campaign ideas.

Mixed tasks

Analyze a file, explain a topic, create a table, draft slides.

Try this prompt

I am a beginner teacher. Give me 5 practical ways AI can save me 30 minutes this week. Use a table with task, AI help, and what I must check.

Best when you need a flexible assistantSlide 6
Based on current ChatGPT feature direction: files, projects, tools, and broad productivity. Keep examples simple.
Tool List 2

Claude is often useful for longer writing and careful editing.

Use it when the task needs reading, rewriting, structured thinking, or a more polished long response.

Long documents

Reports, policy drafts, SOPs, proposals, manuals.

Careful rewrite

Make text clearer, warmer, shorter, or more professional.

Thoughtful review

Find gaps, contradictions, weak logic, or missing sections.

Try this prompt

Review this draft report for clarity, structure, and missing points. Give 3 strengths, 5 improvements, and a revised outline before rewriting.

Best when the answer needs depth and polishSlide 7

Use it when you already work in Word, Excel, PowerPoint, Outlook, Teams, or SharePoint.

Word and Outlook

Excel and data

Custom GPTs and Gems

A custom assistant is a saved helper for a repeated job.

Instead of rewriting the same long prompt every time, you save instructions once. Then the assistant follows those instructions whenever you use it.

Reusable Role-specific No-code Needs testing

Simple examples

  • Teacher Assistant for lesson plans and quizzes
  • Report Writer for office summaries
  • Parent Notice Helper for school messages
  • Social Media Caption Coach for a small business
Custom GPTs are in ChatGPT; Gems are in GeminiSlide 10
Explain that availability depends on account, plan, age, and workspace permissions.
What To Put Inside
Purpose

What job should this assistant do again and again?

Behavior

How should it respond? Ask questions, give tables, be brief?

Knowledge

What documents, rules, examples, or style notes should guide it?

Boundaries

What should it never do? Private data, final grading, legal claims?

Responsible AI

Before sharing a custom assistant, check what is inside.

A GPT or Gem can contain instructions, examples, uploaded files, and behavior rules. Some of that may be private or unsuitable for sharing.

Remove private data

No student names, IDs, marks, salaries, or confidential files.

Check instructions

Do not include passwords, hidden policies, or sensitive internal details.

Share carefully

Private, link, workspace, or public settings may behave differently.

Review outputs

The assistant still needs human review every time.

Recap and Homework

You now have a tool-choice habit and an assistant blueprint.
