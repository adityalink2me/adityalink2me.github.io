Prompt Quality

Weak prompt

Write about AI.

  • No audience
  • No purpose
  • No format
  • No level or tone

Stronger prompt

You are a friendly trainer. Explain generative AI to beginner teachers in Nepal. Use 5 simple bullet points, one classroom example, and a warm tone.

  • Clear role
  • Specific task
  • Useful context
  • Requested format and tone

Use five ingredients for a clearer prompt.

Who should AI act as?

T

What should AI do?

Who is it for and why?

Example: Grade 9 students

F

Format

How should it look?

Example: 5 bullets, table, email

T

Follow-Up Power

The first answer is not the final answer.

Good AI users keep improving the answer. Follow-up prompts are where the real value appears.

Make it simpler

"Rewrite this for beginners using daily-life examples."

Change the format

"Turn this into a table with task, AI help, and human check."

Improve quality

"Find gaps, improve clarity, and ask me 3 questions before finalizing."

Follow-up prompts are normalSlide 10
Tell learners they do not need the perfect first prompt. They can shape the answer step by step.
Prompt Chaining

For bigger work, use small connected steps.

1

Plan

Ask for an outline or checklist first.

2

Draft

Ask AI to create the first version.

3

Review

Ask AI to find gaps, errors, and missing details.

4

Polish

Ask for final tone, format, and clarity.

Example chain: plan a lesson - draft worksheet - make quiz - create answer key - check difficulty.

Quality Trick

Ask AI to improve its own answer.

This is simple and powerful. You can ask AI to act like an editor, reviewer, teacher, or audience member.

Find gaps Ask questions Improve clarity Check tone

Copy-ready follow-up prompts

  1. Review your answer and list 3 ways to improve it.
  2. Ask me 3 questions before writing the final version.
  3. Make the answer simpler, shorter, and more practical.
  4. Check if anything might be wrong or unsupported.
AI can help you edit, but you approve the final workSlide 12
Emphasize that AI's review can also be wrong. Human judgment remains necessary.
Responsible Prompting
Do not paste private data

Names, IDs, passwords, marks, salaries, private files, or confidential records.

Use placeholders

Replace real names with Student A, Client B, or Department X.

Check facts

Verify dates, numbers, laws, policies, and formal claims.

Own the result

AI can draft. You review, edit, and take responsibility.

Recap and Homework

Prompting is a skill you improve by testing.

Start clear, review the output, ask follow-ups, protect private data, and save your best prompts for reuse.

Homework before Day 3

  2. Test 2 prompts in ChatGPT, Claude, or Gemini.
