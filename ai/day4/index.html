Challenge 1 | AI Mind Reader

Your mission

6 min

Choose an everyday object. Describe it to AI without saying its name. Can AI guess it?

I am thinking of an object. Ask me up to 5 yes/no questions, one at a time, then guess the object. Do not guess before asking the questions.

Make it harder

  1. Choose an unusual object.
  2. Give one confusing clue.
  3. Correct AI if its question is unclear.
  4. Ask AI why it made its final guess.
Learn: conversationLearn: clear answers
Share: Which question helped AI most?Slide 4
Excellent for beginners because they only need short answers.
Challenge 2 | Explain It Three Ways

Your mission

8 min

Choose one topic: Wi-Fi, inflation, photosynthesis, online safety, or your profession.

Explain [TOPIC] three ways: 1. To a 7-year-old child 2. To a grandparent 3. To a busy manager Use one analogy for each.

Team check

  • Which explanation is easiest?
  • Which analogy is wrong or weak?
  • Ask AI to improve the weakest one.
  • Create a fourth version for your real audience.
Learn: audienceLearn: tone
Share: Read the funniest useful analogySlide 5
This teaches audience adaptation through play, not theory.
Challenge 3 | Impossible Event Planner

Your mission

10 min

Ask AI to plan an event with difficult constraints.

Plan a fun 90-minute celebration for 25 people. Budget: NPR 1,500. Problems: It may rain, there is no electricity, and 5 people are shy. Give a schedule, materials, budget, and backup plan.

Reality test

  1. Find 3 unrealistic ideas.
  2. Tell AI what is wrong.
  3. Ask for a revised plan.
  4. Add one local activity yourselves.
Learn: constraintsLearn: critical review
Winner: most realistic fun planSlide 6
Give bonus recognition for teams that catch bad assumptions.
Challenge 4 | Bad Advice Detective

AI can sound confident and still be unhelpful.

Your team must catch weak advice, missing questions, and unsafe assumptions.

Your mission

8 min
I feel tired and stressed. Tell me exactly what medical treatment I need.

Run the prompt, then improve it so AI gives safer support without acting like a doctor.

Better direction: Ask for general well-being ideas, warning signs that need professional help, and questions to discuss with a qualified professional.

Find: confidence without evidenceSlide 7
Use a non-personal fictional situation. Emphasize qualified advice for health, legal, and financial matters.
Challenge 5 | Mystery Solver

Your mission

8 min

Make AI solve a harmless mystery by asking questions.

Mystery: Every morning, the classroom floor is wet, but the roof does not leak and nobody cleaned it. Ask me one question at a time. Do not give a final answer until you have enough clues.

Your job

  • Invent the true answer before starting.
  • Answer AI's questions consistently.
  • Notice if AI guesses too early.
  • Ask AI to explain its reasoning.
Learn: asking questionsLearn: reasoning
Share: Did AI solve your mystery?Slide 8
Possible answer: wet umbrellas placed near the classroom after morning rain.
Challenge 6 | Friendly Debate Coach

Your mission

9 min

Choose a harmless topic and make AI prepare both sides.

Topic: Schools should have one homework-free day every week. Give: - 3 arguments for - 3 arguments against - 2 questions each side should answer Do not choose a winner.

Mini debate

  1. Pair A speaks for 45 seconds.
  2. Pair B speaks against for 45 seconds.
  3. Each side asks one AI-generated question.
  4. The room votes on the stronger reasoning.
Learn: balanced thinkingLearn: evaluate arguments
Other topics: uniforms, group work, early classesSlide 9
Keep topics friendly and low-stakes.

Team Mission | Make a Game

Final creation

20 min

Use AI to invent a simple learning game that can be played in the room without paid tools.

Create a 10-minute group game that teaches [TOPIC]. Players: 4 to 6. Materials: paper and pens only. Include rules, scoring, 5 sample questions, and an easy version for beginners.

Required improvements

  • Play-test one round.
  • Find one confusing rule.
  • Ask AI to fix it.
  • Add one original rule from your team.
  • Give the game a memorable name.
