What type of project?

A lesson plan, quiz, CV, office document, report, poster, presentation, research summary, communication kit, portfolio, or any useful work output.

Choosing the Project Keep it practical

A good project starts from a real work problem

1. Pick a task

Choose one task you normally do at school, office, business, or personal career development.

2. Improve it with AI

Use AI to make the task faster, clearer, more organized, or more creative.

3. Show the result

Present the final output and explain how it can be used after the training.

Simple test: If you can use the project next week in your real life or work, it is a good project.

Presentation Basics What to include

Every final presentation should answer these questions

  1. What did you build?
    Show the project name and the final output.
  2. Why did you build it?
    Explain the problem or daily task it solves.
  3. Which AI tools did you use?
    Mention tools like ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Perplexity, NotebookLM, or others.
  4. How did AI help?
    Explain what became faster, easier, clearer, or better.
Clarity matters more than decoration06
Simple Slide Structure 7-slide format

Use this simple format for your final presentation

Slide 1: Title

Project name, your name, and your profession or role.

Slide 2: Problem

What task or challenge did you want to solve?

Slide 3: AI Tools Used

Which tools did you use and why?

Slide 4: Process

Show the steps: prompt, research, draft, edit, design, final check.

Slide 5: Final Output

Show screenshots, document pages, quiz, poster, video, or resource pack.

Slide 6-7: Impact & Learning

Explain how it helps your work and what you learned from the course.

Recommended length: 5-7 minutes07
Content Quality Make slides easy

What makes a presentation good?

Clear title

Each slide should have one simple message.

Short points

Use 3 to 5 bullet points. Avoid long paragraphs.

Real example

Show your actual work, not only theory.

Simple language

Explain like you are helping a friend understand.

A good presentation is not "more text." A good presentation is clear thinking.

I needed a simple way to prepare class notes, quiz questions, and activities for mixed-level students.

Speaking Tips Present with confidence

How to present your project clearly

Start simple

"My project is about..." and "I built this because..."

Show evidence

Display screenshots, documents, posters, quiz pages, or before-and-after examples.

Explain learning

Share what changed in your work after learning AI tools.

Do not read everything

Use slides as support. Speak naturally.

Keep time

Aim for 5 to 7 minutes. Practise once before presenting.

End with next step

Explain how you will continue using the project after training.
