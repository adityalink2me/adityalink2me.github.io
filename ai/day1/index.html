AI is software that can find patterns, make predictions, or create helpful output from examples and instructions.

Patterns Predictions Creation Assistance

Simple Model

AI is like a very fast pattern helper.

1. It receives input

Words, images, files, numbers, voice, or instructions from a person.

->

2. It compares patterns

It uses what it learned from data to predict useful next steps.

->

3. It gives output

Answer, summary, draft, image idea, table, plan, or slide outline.

Simple flow: input - pattern - outputNo programming background needed
Avoid deep ML terms here. The mental model is enough for Day 1.
Key Idea

Generative AI creates new content from your instructions.

It can draft, rewrite, summarize, design, brainstorm, explain, and produce first versions. The human still decides what is correct, useful, respectful, and ready.

Text

Emails, lesson plans, notices, quiz questions, summaries, reports.

Images

Posters, visual ideas, presentation graphics, concept art.

Slides

Outlines, full decks, speaker notes, story flow, visual prompts.

Audio

Voiceover scripts, podcast outlines, pronunciation practice.

Video

Scripts, scenes, captions, storyboard plans, short-form ideas.

Data

Tables, formulas, charts, dashboard ideas, cleanup suggestions.

Main message: AI makes drafts, people make decisionsSlide 5
Use "first draft machine" carefully: it helps, but it is not always correct. Emphasize review.

Chatbots are conversation-based AI assistants.

They respond to prompts, remember the recent conversation, accept follow-up questions, and can change tone, format, or level when asked.

Responsible Use Starter
Do not share private data

Passwords, personal IDs, confidential school or office records, private student details.

Check facts

AI can sound confident while being wrong. Verify names, dates, numbers, laws, and policies.

Edit the output

Make the answer match your audience, local context, and real purpose.

Use human judgment

AI supports decisions. It should not be the only decision-maker for important matters.

Safe habit: remove, check, edit, decideSlide 15
Keep this positive: safety is a professional habit, not a fear message.

Sharing Round

Turn task ideas into better AI use cases.

Share one task with a partner. Improve it together using these three questions.

What is the exact output?

Email, table, slide outline, checklist, quiz, poster copy, or plan?

What context matters?

Audience, class level, office role, language, deadline, format, constraints.

What must a human check?

Facts, privacy, tone, fairness, school or office rules, final quality.

Recap and Homework

AI is a practical assistant, not magic.

It helps with drafts, ideas, summaries, organization, and creative starting points. The person using it stays responsible for the final result.

Homework before Day 2

  1. Finalize your list of 5 AI-helpful tasks.
  2. Pick one task you want to try with a chatbot.
  3. Bring one "bad prompt" and one "better prompt" idea.

You do not need to become an AI engineer. You are learning to become a more confident teacher, professional, and creator with AI support.
