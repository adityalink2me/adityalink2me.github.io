Opening Game

Which message sounds more human?

It is with immense pleasure that I am writing to inform you about our upcoming educational program. This remarkable initiative will undoubtedly provide invaluable opportunities for all participants.
Vote, then explain your reason.

Look for clues

  • Generic praise without evidence
  • Formal words nobody in the room uses
  • Repeated sentence structure
  • No concrete people, place, time, or action
  • Perfectly smooth but forgettable language
Robotic writing can come from humans tooSlide 2
Do not claim that style proves AI authorship.
Better Definition
Specific

Includes the details the audience actually needs.

Personal

Reflects the writer's real opinion, knowledge, or experience.

Natural

Uses words, rhythm, and tone the writer would normally use.

Responsible

Does not invent experiences, facts, feelings, or sources.

Humanizing is not adding random mistakes. It is making the message clear, honest, useful, and recognizably yours.

Authentic beats artificially imperfectSlide 3
Challenge the myth that spelling mistakes make writing human.

Five Editing Moves

Turn a generic draft into your message.

1Cut

Remove filler, repetition, and exaggerated claims.

2Specify

Add real dates, people, actions, examples, and limits.

3Personalize

Add your view, experience, or reason.

4Speak

Use words and sentence rhythm natural to you.

5Verify

Check facts and remove anything AI invented.

Cut, specify, personalize, speak, verifySlide 5
Have everyone repeat the five editing moves.

AI Detector Reality Check

Detection scores are clues, not proof.

Different detectors may disagree. Genuine human writing can be flagged, while edited AI writing may not be flagged.

Before You Share

Use the VOICE checklist.

VVerify

Check facts and remove invented information.

OOwn

Can you explain and stand behind every sentence?

IInclude

Add real details, context, opinion, or experience.

CCut

Remove filler, clichés, repetition, and exaggeration.

EEdit aloud

Read it aloud. Does it sound like something you would say?

Verify, Own, Include, Cut, Edit aloudSlide 14
Reading aloud is an easy and powerful editing technique.
